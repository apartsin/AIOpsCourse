Week 5   Part II · DataOps · Presentation 1 · Specification

Data Quality, Contracts & Feature Stores

Instructor lesson plan: lecture (2 h) and practice (2 h).

Learning objectives

  • Enforce data quality continuously with validation rules.
  • Define a data contract between a producer and a consumer.
  • Explain feature stores and the cause of train/serve skew.

Common misconception to confront.

Students often think: Data validation is a one-time cleaning step.
Set it straight: Quality is a continuous contract checked on every run. Upstream producers keep changing, so validation must guard the boundary forever, not once.

Check for understanding (pose during the concept blocks; let students answer before revealing).
What is train/serve skew and one cause?
The features seen at serving differ from those at training, e.g. a transformation applied only in the training notebook, or time leakage in offline features.
What does a data contract specify?
The schema, semantics, freshness, and quality guarantees a producer promises a consumer, enforced automatically at the boundary.
Key takeaways.
  • Data quality is a continuous contract, not a one-off clean.
  • Contracts make producer-consumer guarantees explicit and enforced.
  • Train/serve skew is a top cause of silent model failure.

🗣️Practice · 2 hours · student presentations

The full two-hour practice slot is given over to student project presentations (Presentation 1 · Specification). There is no instructor-prepared material: teams present and defend their work to the class, with peer and instructor questions after each talk. Each team has 12 to 15 minutes plus questions, and submits a short written report and a tagged release of the repository.

What each team presents.
  • Problem statement and success metrics (SLOs).
  • System and data architecture.
  • DevOps and DataOps plan: repository, CI skeleton, and data sources.
  • Risk and governance register.

See the running-project brief for the full milestone description and the grading weight.
